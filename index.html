THE STARRY NIGHT

VINCENT VAN GOGH (1889)

Van Gogh's Starry Night is one of the most most famous paintings in post-impressionist culture. It is currently on display at the Museum of Modern Art in New York. Van Gogh was in the hospital of Saint-Remy, tormented by fits of madness.


THE SCREAM

EDVARD MUNCH (1893)

The Scream is an iconic work, one of the most famous paintings, of Norwegian expressionist Edvard Munch. This painting, now in the National Gallery and at the Munch Museum in Oslo, is one of the first in a style in which realism is minimized to give more freedom to emotions.
